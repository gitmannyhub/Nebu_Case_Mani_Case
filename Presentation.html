🅲 Part C: Replenishment & Planning Deficit

Mathematical modeling of network inventory positions and production MOQ rules

🧮 Operational Calculation Inputs

  • Weekly Demand Run-Rate: 1,673 units per week.
  • Target Safety Cover Buffer: Strict 12-week network safety target requires 20,076 units in position.
  • True Inventory Position Formula: On Hand (8,000) + Open PO (10,000) - Backlog (9,500) = 8,500 net units.
  • Net Deficit: 20,076 (Target) - 8,500 (Position) = 11,576 units raw deficit.

🛒 MOQ Step-Batch Ceiling Function

Dividing the 11,576 deficit by the strict 5,000-unit factory MOQ block yields 2.315 batches.

Applying a mathematical ceiling function rounds the procurement proposal up to 3 full production batches, generating an optimized 15,000-unit New Buy order. This completely insulates the peak 9,000-unit demand spike hitting the floor in Month 3.

💼 Justification to Finance

Ordering only 10,000 units would fail to clear the deficit, leaving a critical 1,576-unit exposure gap. The 15,000-unit order protects high-margin revenue and market share during the critical New product lifecycle phase where stockout costs far outweigh localized holding expenses.

🅳 Part D: Operating Model & Governance Architecture

Documented Standard Operating Procedures, cross-functional ownership, and risk reduction

📋 Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Sequence

Step 1: Alert Trigger (Automated ERP tracking flags a production constraint at CMHK) ➔ Step 2: Split Validation (Procurement confirms exact split quantities and arrival windows within 24 hours) ➔ Step 3: Asset Processing (Planning applies net asset formulas to compute deficits against safety cover) ➔ Step 4: Priority Cascade (Allocation engine triggers priority commercial cascade protecting launch lines) ➔ Step 5: Governance Loop (Procurement executes step-batch reorders through appropriate authorization gates).

🚦 Weeks of Supply (WOS) Escalation Triggers

  • 🟢 Normal (WOS >= 12): Routine automated monitoring handled completely by the Supply Planner.
  • 🟡 Planner Review (WOS < 8): Flagged constraint window; local 3PL rebalancing activated by the Supply Planner.
  • 🟠 Management Sync (WOS < 6): Daily tracking cycles and cross-functional synchronization led by the Supply Planning Manager.
  • 🔴 Executive Isolation (WOS < 4): Emergency meeting with CMHK leadership driven directly by the Operations Director.

🌍 Long-Term Global Risk Mitigation

  • Supplier Diversification: Establish a formal dual-sourcing strategy by qualifying a secondary manufacturing node outside Hong Kong to remove single-source exposure.
  • Safety Stock Calibration: Transition to differentiated inventory models: holding higher component buffers for New lines while keeping mature items lean.
  • Inventory Positioning: Position inventory nodes closer to regional 3PL hubs (US/EU) to compress transit legs and absorb unexpected demand spikes.

📧 Part E: Quality & Returns Management

Real-time exception workflows, automated quarantines, and rapid customer response pipelines

🚨 Real-Time Outlier Detection & Quarantining

  • Z-Score Monitoring Dashboard: Configured to process regional return log data fields continuously. The absolute millisecond returns cross a strict 1.5% defect threshold, an emergency system hold triggers.
  • Instant Gateway Isolation: Systemic hold codes execute immediately across the ERP architecture, freezing and quarantining remaining lots at the central HK111 gateway and regional 3PL networks before defective items reach downstream customers.

⚡ Accelerated RMA Pipeline

  • Scrap-in-Place Protocol: Distributors log failing serial strings via an online portal. To bypass expensive cross-border reverse logistics freight and customs friction, regional 3PLs execute an immediate local scrap-in-place protocol within a 15-minute resolution SLA, auto-generating instant credit memos.

Mandate automated optical inspection loops on the factory floor, enforce strict component screening gates at intake, and implement isolated pilot-run verification cycles on all subsequent production lots.
